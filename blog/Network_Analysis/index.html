What is a network?

Networks have become a standard tool for analyzing the relationships/interactions between the components of a complex system. When it comes to understanding and modeling certain systems, like protein-protein interactions or gene regulatory networks, it becomes apparent that these systems portray non-linear behavior and are simply too complex to model with a simple mathematical equation.

By utilizing a branch of mathematics known as graph theory, we can produce simple visual representations of these kinds of systems by treating the components of the system as vertices and the relationships between them as edges.
